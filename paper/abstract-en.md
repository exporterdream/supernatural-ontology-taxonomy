# Abstract: Supernatural Ontology Taxonomy — The Bi-Axial Model of Source and Dimension

**Author**: Zihan Tian 
**Date**: 2026-06-26  
**Keywords**: Supernatural Ontology, Monster Taxonomy, Folklore Studies, ACGN, Bi-Axial Model, Cultural Translation

---

## Abstract

Traditional folklore classifications of supernatural beings (ghosts, demons, spirits, gods), while coherent within their original cultural contexts, are often applied beyond their intended scope when used for cross-cultural comparative analysis. As Jeffrey Jerome Cohen (1996) argues in *Monster Theory*, "monsters" are fundamentally cultural constructs—projections of the "Other" onto the boundaries of human cognition. Yet when these boundaries shift across time, geography, and narrative genres, traditional taxonomies can generate confusion rather than clarity.

Consider the following case: the classical Chinese "ghost" (鬼, guǐ) denotes a soul-based, semi-corporeal entity (e.g., Nie Xiaoqian from *Strange Tales from a Chinese Studio*). However, in the contemporary Japanese anime *Demon Slayer*, the antagonist Muzan is labeled a "ghost" (鬼, oni) while possessing a fully physical, virus-based biology—a fundamental ontological mismatch that conventional systems cannot resolve without acknowledging that the label itself has migrated across cultural universes.

This paper proposes a novel **Bi-Axial Model** to resolve this chaos through two independent axes:

**Axis 1 — The Source (S)**: Categorizes the ontological origin into four codes:
- **S-Nat** (Nature/Essence): born from natural energy or cosmic essence
- **S-Soul** (Psyche/Karma): born from obsession, resentment, or votive faith
- **S-Bio** (Biology/Virus): born from DNA, cellular mutation, or infection
- **S-Cos** (Cosmic/Outer God): originating from higher dimensions or extra-universal entities

**Axis 2 — The Dimension (D)**: Categorizes physical manifestation into four states:
- **D-0** (Pure Ethereal): completely invisible, only perceivable through dreams or possession
- **D-1** (Semi-corporeal): visible but lacks physical substance (mist, shadow)
- **D-2** (Solid Physical): possesses a fully tangible body susceptible to physical harm
- **D-3** (Avatar/Projection): the true entity exists in a higher dimension but can project avatars

Every supernatural entity is defined by the formula **Entity = (Source, Dimension)**. For example:
- Nie Xiaoqian = (S-Soul, D-1)
- Dracula = (S-Bio, D-2)
- Cthulhu = (S-Cos, D-0/D-3)

By applying this framework to canonical cases across Chinese folklore (*Journey to the West*, *Strange Tales*), Western literature (*Dracula*, Lovecraft's Cthulhu Mythos), modern cinema (Romero's zombies, *Mr. Vampire*), and contemporary ACGN (*Demon Slayer*), this model demonstrates a **paradigm shift**: from a static, "taxidermy" approach (what is it by name?) to a dynamic, "evolutionary stage" approach (what is its ontological constitution and manifestation?).

The paper argues that the confusion in supernatural classification arises not from the entities themselves, but from the **cultural translation** (Asad, 1986) and **semantic drift** (Ullmann, 1962) of labels when they migrate across cultural contexts and narrative genres. The Bi-Axial Model offers a universal, cross-cultural tool for scholars, writers, and world-builders to precisely locate any supernatural being—regardless of its cultural origin or narrative era—on a clear, extensible coordinate system. By providing a common analytical language while respecting the internal coherence of each tradition, it facilitates cross-cultural dialogue about supernatural imaginaries without imposing a single cultural standard.
